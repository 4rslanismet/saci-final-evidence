Important interpretation note

SACI=100 does not mean that the environment is secure. It only means that the expected visibility relationships were observed within the defined evaluation scope. The explanation layer does not calculate or modify SACI scores; it only explains deterministic outputs produced by the SACI engine.

Scenario taxonomy

Scenario group Scenario IDs Purpose
Baseline / limited visibility S0-S2 Shows the initial low-visibility state before meaningful telemetry and inventory coverage are established.
Visibility progression S3-S6 Demonstrates how SACI increases as domain controller, endpoint, firewall and CTI visibility are introduced.
Criticality regression S7A-S7B Compares critical and non-critical telemetry loss. Critical DC01 Sysmon loss produces a stronger regression.
Final closure S8 Represents complete expected visibility closure within the defined evaluation scope.
Post-closure sensitivity S9-S16 Injects isolated visibility faults after final closure. Scores may remain high when only limited or low-weight relationships are affected.
Recovery and scope validation S17-S18 Validates recovery after fix and confirms that legacy out-of-scope controls do not incorrectly reduce the final score.

Why some post-closure scores remain high

S9-S18 scenarios are not catastrophic outage scenarios. They are isolated post-closure fault-injection and sensitivity cases. Therefore, SACI scores remain relatively high when only a limited number of visibility relationships or a low-weight component is affected. Stronger visibility regression is demonstrated in S7A, where critical DC01 Sysmon telemetry loss reduces SACI to 78.53 and produces 57 missing visibility edges.
